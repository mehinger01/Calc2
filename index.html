Spaced retrieval practice

6.1 recall: Without looking at your notes, write out the formula for the area between two curves f(x) and g(x) on [a, b]. What condition must hold for the formula to work directly?

Area = ∫ₐᵇ [f(x) − g(x)] dx, where f(x) ≥ g(x) on [a,b]. If they cross, split the integral at each crossing point and use the absolute value of the difference on each sub-interval.

6.2 recall: Describe in words (not formula) how the disk method builds a solid of revolution. What does each "disk" represent and what is its volume?

Each disk is a thin circular slice of the solid, perpendicular to the axis of rotation. Its radius is f(x) and its thickness is dx. Volume of one disk ≈ π[f(x)]² dx. Integrate to sum them all: V = π∫[f(x)]² dx.

6.3 recall: The arc length formula contains a square root with a derivative inside. Write it from memory and explain why the derivative appears there.

L = ∫ₐᵇ √(1 + [f′(x)]²) dx. The f′(x) term appears because arc length uses the Pythagorean theorem on tiny segments: ds² = dx² + dy², and dy = f′(x)dx, so ds = √(1 + [f′(x)]²) dx.

Mixed 6.1–6.2: Find the area between y = x² and y = x on [0,1]. Then set up (don't solve) the integral for the volume when this region is rotated around the x-axis using the washer method.

Area: ∫₀¹ (x − x²) dx = [x²/2 − x³/3]₀¹ = 1/2 − 1/3 = 1/6.

Washer volume: V = π∫₀¹ [(x)² − (x²)²] dx = π∫₀¹ [x² − x⁴] dx. Outer radius R = x, inner radius r = x², since x ≥ x² on [0,1].

Conceptual: Why does the washer method use R² − r² rather than (R − r)²? Draw a quick sketch to explain the difference.

The washer's area is πR² − πr² (outer disk minus inner disk). (R−r)² would give the area of a solid disk with radius equal to the gap width — a completely different shape. The subtraction happens after squaring, never before.

Transfer problem (interleaved with Ch. 5): A region is bounded by y = √x, y = 0, and x = 4. Use the shell method to find the volume when this region is rotated around the y-axis. How does your setup differ from the disk method?

Shell method: V = 2π∫₀⁴ x·√x dx = 2π∫₀⁴ x^(3/2) dx = 2π · [2x^(5/2)/5]₀⁴ = 2π · (64/5) = 128π/5.

The shell method integrates in x but rotates around the y-axis — each shell has radius x, height √x, thickness dx. The disk method around the y-axis would require rewriting everything in terms of y.

Synthesis: How are the area formula (6.1), the disk/washer formula (6.2), and the arc length formula (6.3) all really the same idea applied differently? What is the underlying pattern?

All three slice a quantity into infinitesimally thin pieces, express each piece as a simple formula, then integrate to sum across the interval. Area slices a 2D region into thin rectangles. Volume slices a 3D solid into thin disks or shells. Arc length slices a curve into tiny straight segments using the Pythagorean theorem. The integration is always "add up infinitely many infinitely small pieces."

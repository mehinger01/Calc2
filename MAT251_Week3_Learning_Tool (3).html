Before class
Why does this matter?

When NASA engineers calculated how much fuel the Saturn V rocket needed to escape Earth's gravity, they used integration — specifically work against a variable force. The rocket's weight decreases as fuel burns, so force isn't constant. This week you'll learn exactly how to handle that.

What we're covering

6.4 — Work done by a variable force. W = F·d only works when force is constant. For springs, chains, pumping — force changes at every point. The fix: W = ∫ₐᵇ F(x) dx. Hooke's Law: F(x) = kx.

Key examples: Stretching a spring, lifting a chain, pumping water out of a tank.

6.5 — Average value. Average of a list: sum ÷ count. For a function over [a,b]: f_avg = 1/(b−a) · ∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx. The Mean Value Theorem for Integrals guarantees f actually hits this average somewhere on [a,b].

Real-world: Average temperature, average velocity, average drug concentration.

6.6 — Center of mass. The balance point of a flat region. Moment about y-axis: Mᵧ = ρ∫x·f(x)dx. Total mass m = ρ∫f(x)dx. Centroid: x̄ = Mᵧ/m. For uniform density, ρ cancels.

Intuition: Where would you balance a shaped piece of cardboard on your finger?

6.7 — Hydrostatic force. Pressure increases with depth: P = ρgd. Force on a submerged surface: F = ∫ ρg·d(y)·w(y) dy — because pressure varies at different depths, you can't just multiply a single pressure by the total area.

Why it matters: Dam walls, submarine hulls, underwater windows — all designed using this integral.

Spaced retrieval — free-response

6.4: A spring stretches 0.2 m under 20 N. Write the work integral to stretch it from natural length to 0.4 m additional stretch. Find k first.

k = F/x = 20/0.2 = 100 N/m. W = ∫₀^0.4 100x dx = 100[x²/2]₀^0.4 = 50(0.16) = 8 J.

6.5: Write the average value formula and state the Mean Value Theorem for Integrals in one sentence.

f_avg = 1/(b−a)·∫ₐᵇf(x)dx. MVT for Integrals: If f is continuous on [a,b], there exists c ∈ [a,b] where f(c) = f_avg.

6.6: In words: what does the moment of a region about the y-axis measure, and why is x multiplied in its formula?

Moment measures rotational tendency about the axis. Strips far from the axis "pull harder" on the balance point — like sitting far from a seesaw pivot. The x weights each area strip by its distance from the y-axis.

6.7: Write the hydrostatic force formula for a vertical submerged plate. Define each variable.

F = ∫ₐᵇ ρg·d(y)·w(y) dy. ρ = fluid density, g = gravity, d(y) = depth of strip at y, w(y) = width of plate at y. Each thin strip has force ≈ pressure × area = ρg·d·w dy.

Mixed 6.4–6.5: A 10 m chain weighs 50 N/m. Find the work to lift it all to the top, then find the average force during the lift.

W = ∫₀^10 50(10−x)dx = 50[10x−x²/2]₀^10 = 50(50) = 2500 J. Average force = W/distance = 2500/10 = 250 N.

Conceptual 6.6: A region bounded by y = x² and y = 4 on [−2,2]. Without computing, predict x̄. Then state what integral you would compute to verify it.

x̄ = 0 — the region is symmetric about the y-axis. To verify: Mᵧ = ρ∫₋₂² x(4−x²)dx. Since x(4−x²) is an odd function, this integral = 0 over a symmetric interval. So x̄ = 0/m = 0.

Transfer — conical tank: A cone (point down) has radius 3 m at top, height 6 m, full of water (ρ = 1000 kg/m³). Set up (don't evaluate) the work integral to pump all water over the rim.

At height y from bottom: radius = y/2 (similar triangles). Lift distance = 6−y. W = ∫₀⁶ 1000(9.8)·π(y/2)²·(6−y) dy = 2450π∫₀⁶ y²(6−y) dy.

Exam-style mixed problems

Problem 1 (6.1): Find the area enclosed by y = sin x and y = cos x on [π/4, 5π/4].

On [π/4,5π/4], sin x ≥ cos x. ∫_{π/4}^{5π/4}(sin x−cos x)dx = [−cos x−sin x] = (√2/2+√2/2)−(−√2/2−√2/2) = 2√2.

Problem 2 (6.2): Region bounded by y = √x and y = x rotated about x-axis. Find volume (washer method).

Intersect: x = 0,1. On [0,1]: R = √x, r = x. V = π∫₀¹(x−x²)dx = π[x²/2−x³/3]₀¹ = π/6.

Problem 3 (6.4): A 20 kg bucket hangs from a 10 m rope (2 kg/m). Find total work to haul everything up.

Bucket: W₁ = 20(9.8)(10) = 1960 J. Rope: W₂ = ∫₀^10 2(9.8)(10−x)dx = 19.6[10x−x²/2]₀^10 = 980 J. Total = 2940 J.

Problem 4 (6.5): T(t) = 68 + 4sin(πt/8) over 8 hours. Find average temperature.

T_avg = (1/8)∫₀⁸[68+4sin(πt/8)]dt = (1/8)[68t−(32/π)cos(πt/8)]₀⁸ = 68 + 8/π ≈ 70.55°.

Problem 5 (6.7): Square plate 2m × 2m, top edge 1 m below surface. Set up hydrostatic force integral.

Depth ranges 1 to 3 m; width = 2 m throughout. F = ∫₁³ 1000(9.8)(y)(2) dy = 19600∫₁³ y dy = 19600[y²/2]₁³ = 19600(4) = 78,400 N.

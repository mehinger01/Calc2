Week 2: Applications of Integration

Net change, velocity, position, and area between curves

Mission for the Week

Use this organizer before the week begins, then revisit it after each study session. The goal is not just to “see” the topic, but to recognize the problem type, choose a method, and explain the reasoning.

DisplacementDistance TraveledFuture ValueArea Between Curves

Big Ideas

  • An integral accumulates change.
  • Velocity integrates to displacement.
  • Speed integrates to total distance.
  • Rates connect to future values.

Formula / Tool Bank

Displacement: ∫[a,b] v(t) dt
Distance Traveled: ∫[a,b] |v(t)| dt
Future Value: Q(t) = Q(0) + ∫[0,t] Q′(x) dx
Area Between Curves: A = ∫[a,b] (upper − lower) dx

Understanding Checks

  • ☐ Can I tell whether the problem asks for displacement or distance?
  • ☐ Can I split an integral where velocity changes sign?
  • ☐ Can I write a future-value equation from a rate?
  • ☐ Can I describe what the units of the integral mean?

Common Mistakes to Watch

  • Treating distance and displacement as the same.
  • Ignoring negative area below the axis.
  • Not using absolute value for speed.
  • Forgetting initial value in accumulation problems.

Reteach Loop

If stuck: pause, rewrite the problem type in plain English, identify the target quantity, circle the given information, and choose the method before doing any algebra.

  1. What kind of problem is this?
  2. What is the target: value, area, volume, convergence, slope, or model?
  3. What formula or test fits?
  4. What would a wrong setup look like?
  5. Can I explain the first step out loud?

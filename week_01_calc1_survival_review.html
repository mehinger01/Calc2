L’Hôpital’s Rule, u-substitution, FTC, and area between curves

Big Ideas

  • L’Hôpital’s Rule only works for indeterminate forms.
  • The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus turns definite integrals into function values.
  • u-substitution reverses the chain rule.
  • Area between curves is top minus bottom, or right minus left.

Formula / Tool Bank

L’Hôpital’s Rule: If lim f/g is 0/0 or ∞/∞, then lim f/g = lim f′/g′, if that new limit exists.
Fundamental Theorem: ∫[a,b] f(x) dx = F(b) − F(a)
u-substitution: Let u = inside function; du must match the leftover factor.
Area Between Curves: A = ∫[a,b] (top − bottom) dx

Understanding Checks

  • ☐ Can I confirm the limit is indeterminate before using L’Hôpital?
  • ☐ Can I choose u and rewrite du correctly?
  • ☐ Can I identify top and bottom from a graph or equations?
  • ☐ Can I evaluate a definite integral cleanly?

Common Mistakes to Watch

  • Using L’Hôpital too early.
  • Forgetting +C on indefinite integrals.
  • Forgetting to change bounds in u-substitution.
  • Subtracting bottom minus top.

Reteach Loop

Week 8: Power Series, Parametric, and Polar

Power series, Taylor ideas, parametric curves, and polar coordinates

Mission for the Week

Use this organizer before the week begins, then revisit it after each study session. The goal is not just to “see” the topic, but to recognize the problem type, choose a method, and explain the reasoning.

Power SeriesRadius/IntervalParametric SlopePolar Conversion

Big Ideas

  • Power series are functions written as infinite polynomials.
  • Radius of convergence comes first; endpoints must be checked separately.
  • Parametric curves use time/parameter to create x and y.
  • Polar coordinates describe points by distance and angle.

Formula / Tool Bank

Power Series: Σ cₙ(x−a)ⁿ
Radius/Interval: Use ratio/root test, then check endpoints.
Parametric Slope: dy/dx = (dy/dt)/(dx/dt)
Polar Conversion: x = r cosθ, y = r sinθ, r² = x² + y²

Understanding Checks

  • ☐ Can I find radius before interval?
  • ☐ Can I test both endpoints separately?
  • ☐ Can I compute dy/dx for parametric equations?
  • ☐ Can I convert between polar and rectangular coordinates?

Common Mistakes to Watch

  • Forgetting endpoint tests.
  • Treating t like x in parametric slope.
  • Losing orientation on parametric curves.
  • Confusing r and θ in polar graphs.

Reteach Loop

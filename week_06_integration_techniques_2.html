Week 6: Integration Techniques II

Trig substitution, partial fractions, numerical methods, improper integrals, and differential equations

Mission for the Week

Use this organizer before the week begins, then revisit it after each study session. The goal is not just to “see” the topic, but to recognize the problem type, choose a method, and explain the reasoning.

Trig SubstitutionPartial FractionsImproper IntegralSeparable DE

Big Ideas

  • Trig substitution handles radicals with special forms.
  • Partial fractions breaks rational functions into simpler pieces.
  • Improper integrals require limits.
  • Differential equations connect functions to their rates of change.

Formula / Tool Bank

Trig Substitution: √(a²−x²): x=a sinθ; √(a²+x²): x=a tanθ; √(x²−a²): x=a secθ
Partial Fractions: Factor → decompose → solve coefficients → integrate
Improper Integral: Replace infinite/discontinuous bound with a limit.
Separable DE: dy/dx = g(x)h(y) → separate variables.

Understanding Checks

  • ☐ Can I match the radical to the right trig substitution?
  • ☐ Can I factor the denominator completely?
  • ☐ Can I identify why an integral is improper?
  • ☐ Can I separate variables correctly?

Common Mistakes to Watch

  • Forgetting the triangle after trig substitution.
  • Skipping polynomial division when needed.
  • Evaluating improper integrals without limits.
  • Mixing x and y terms in a differential equation.

Reteach Loop

If stuck: pause, rewrite the problem type in plain English, identify the target quantity, circle the given information, and choose the method before doing any algebra.

  1. What kind of problem is this?
  2. What is the target: value, area, volume, convergence, slope, or model?
  3. What formula or test fits?
  4. What would a wrong setup look like?
  5. Can I explain the first step out loud?
